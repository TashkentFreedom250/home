250 Years of History

The Road to Freedom

From a declaration of independence to a beacon of democracy — milestones that shaped a nation.

1776
Declaration of Independence
Fifty-six delegates sign the founding document, declaring independence and enshrining unalienable rights.
1787
The Constitution Ratified
The world's oldest written national constitution establishes a framework of checks and balances.
1865
End of the Civil War
The 13th Amendment abolishes slavery, beginning a long march toward equal freedom for all.
1920
Women's Suffrage
The 19th Amendment guarantees women the right to vote, expanding democracy's reach.
1969
Moon Landing
"One giant leap for mankind" — America reaches beyond Earth, pushing the frontier of human possibility.
2026
America at 250
A nation celebrates a quarter-millennium of freedom, unity, and the unfinished work of liberty.

Pillars of American Freedom

The principles that have guided the American experiment for 250 years.

✍️
Freedom of Speech

The First Amendment guarantees every American the right to speak, assemble, and petition — the cornerstone of a free society.

⚖️
Rule of Law

No person stands above the law. Equal justice under the law has been America's compass through every challenge.

👪
E Pluribus Unum

Out of many, one. America's greatest strength has always been the diversity of its people united by common ideals.

Innovation & Progress

From the printing press to the internet, American ingenuity has driven the world forward for 250 years.
